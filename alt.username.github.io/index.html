I was raised by a family that voted for Trump. And when you look like this (i.e. brown), that has consequences. I also received an extra-ordinary education; but I have not finished college. This is not to say that I have not had significant successes in my life; though the difficulties are more evident it would seem. I arrive at your door, because I want to learn how to build JS websites, and I want help getting a job. To me, $15,000 is worth being able to get a job that will start out paying me $50,000. While I have all the requisite skills to be very successful in this field. And it hasn't been until now that our culture has been ready to give an individual such as myself a equal opportunity such as this.

University of Minnesota

B.I.S. Economics / Statistics / Communications

I was instructed that I was supposed to go to college after high school; that's what was expected of me, so I gave a go at it. I got distracted and had a child, so, oops. I returned to the University many years later in an attempt to finish. Well, actually I couldn't get a job that paid me enough to live, so I thought I'd take out a few loans and try my hand at that again. I never took out any private loans, thank god; but the experience was as I've always remembered school. I'm usually the only person of color in the room.

I was able to wrangle a job out of the experience, for a time, until my fortuitiousness as a guinea pig ran out. None the less, I was able to form many positive relationships, and obtain informative experiences. I'm about 4 classes from completing my degree, but financial impairment constrains me.

Orono High School

You ever play Duck Duck Brown Duck?

I was one of five student of color in my graduating class. In kindergarden we use to play "duck duck brown duck." It was a traumatic experience to say the least. But I learned how to read, write, and do my arithmetics. And I was also taught how to think critically by the few teachers to dared to invest their time in me. From physics to history, I was a passionate student. Truly these experiences have made me who I am today.

I want to capitalize on my network, but I need the credibility mentorship through Prime can give me.

Presently I sit on the Public Policy board for the MetroIBA

Casey from Software for Good as well as Jay Haas from Clockwork, are helping me develop MnActivist; slowly but surely.

People know of me, but few actually know me. Sadly this is the product of a culturally broken and disconnected society. I would like to help this, but need help myself. This is why I seek entrance to Prime Academy and the doors it can help open for me.
